# Breach Notification Letter Templates

> **Reference**: 45 CFR § 164.404(c) — Content requirements for individual breach notifications
> **See also**: `docs/09-breach-notification-procedure.md` for the full notification procedure.

---

## Instructions for Use

1. Complete the incident investigation fully before sending any notification.
2. Have Legal Counsel review the draft letter before sending.
3. Have the Privacy Officer or Executive Director approve the final version.
4. Send via first-class mail to each individual's last known address. Email is permitted only if the individual has previously agreed to receive communications electronically.
5. Retain a copy of each letter sent and the mailing/delivery records for 6 years.
6. Log each notification in the breach incident log.

---

## Template 1: Standard Individual Breach Notification Letter

---

[Organization Letterhead]

[Date]

[Individual Name]
[Address Line 1]
[Address Line 2]
[City, State ZIP]

**RE: Important Notice Regarding Your Personal Health Information**

Dear [Individual Name],

We are writing to inform you of a security incident that may have affected some of your personal health information. We take the privacy and security of your information very seriously, and we want to make sure you have the information you need to protect yourself.

**What Happened**

On [date of discovery], we discovered that [brief, plain-language description of what occurred — e.g., "an employee's email account was accessed by an unauthorized person," "a laptop was stolen from our office," "a system configuration error temporarily allowed unauthorized access to our records system"]. We immediately took steps to stop the incident and launched an investigation.

Our investigation determined that the incident occurred on or around [estimated date of breach or date range], and that it may have involved your information.

**What Information Was Involved**

The information that may have been affected includes:

- [ ] Your name
- [ ] Date of birth
- [ ] Address
- [ ] Phone number
- [ ] Health/medical information
- [ ] Mental health information
- [ ] Substance use treatment information
- [ ] Social Security Number
- [ ] Other: _______________________________________________

[Delete items that do not apply. Be specific about what was involved.]

**What We Are Doing**

Since discovering this incident, we have taken the following steps:

- [Action 1: e.g., "Secured the affected system and confirmed no further unauthorized access is possible"]
- [Action 2: e.g., "Launched a full investigation with our security team"]
- [Action 3: e.g., "Notified the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services as required by law"]
- [Action 4: e.g., "Implemented additional security controls to prevent a similar incident"]

We are committed to protecting your information and are taking this matter very seriously.

**What You Can Do**

We recommend you take the following steps to help protect your information:

1. **Monitor your accounts**: Review your financial accounts, insurance explanations of benefits, and credit reports for any unusual activity.
2. **Review your medical records**: Contact your health insurance provider and healthcare providers to verify that your records are accurate.
3. **Consider a credit freeze or fraud alert**: You can contact the three major credit bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion) to place a free credit freeze or fraud alert on your file.
4. **Be alert to phishing**: Be cautious of unsolicited emails, phone calls, or text messages asking for personal information, even if they appear to come from organizations you know.

[If SSN was involved, add]:
Because your Social Security Number was involved, we recommend you visit IdentityTheft.gov (the FTC's official site) for guidance on steps to take if you believe your identity has been misused.

**Contact Us**

If you have questions about this notice or would like more information about what happened, please contact us:

[Organization Name] — HIPAA Privacy Office
Phone: [toll-free number], available [hours, days]
Email: [privacy@organization.org]
Mailing address: [full address]

We sincerely apologize for this incident and for any concern it may cause you. We remain committed to protecting your privacy and the security of your information.

Sincerely,

_______________________________________________
[Name]
[Title — e.g., Executive Director / Privacy Officer]
[Organization Name]
[Date]

## Template 2: Brief Substitute Notice (Website Posting — ≥ 10 Individuals with Insufficient Contact Info)

Post prominently on the organization's homepage for a minimum of 90 days.

---

**NOTICE OF DATA SECURITY INCIDENT**

[Organization Name] is providing notice of a recent security incident that may have affected the personal health information of some of our clients.

**What happened**: On [discovery date], we discovered [brief description]. We immediately took steps to contain the incident and launched an investigation.

**What information may have been affected**: [List types of PHI involved]

**What we are doing**: We have taken steps to secure our systems and prevent future incidents, including [brief description of remediation actions]. We have notified the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services.

**What you should do**: If you believe you may be affected, or if you have questions, please contact our Privacy Office at [phone number] or [email address]. We are available [hours and days].

We take the security of your information seriously and sincerely apologize for any concern this may cause.

Posted: [Date]
[Organization Name] | [Address] | [Phone] | [Email]

## Template 3: Media Notification Statement (≥ 500 Individuals in a State)

For use with local news outlets. Provide this statement to news desks; be available for follow-up questions.

---

**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**
Contact: [Name, Title, Phone, Email]
Date: [Date]

**[ORGANIZATION NAME] NOTIFIES CLIENTS OF DATA SECURITY INCIDENT**

[City, State] — [Organization Name], a [type of organization] serving [description of client population], today announced a data security incident that may have affected the personal health information of approximately [number] clients.

On [discovery date], [Organization Name] discovered that [plain-language description of incident]. Upon discovery, the organization immediately [containment actions]. An investigation was promptly launched.

The investigation determined that the incident may have affected clients' [types of PHI involved]. [Organization Name] has notified affected individuals by mail and has reported the incident to the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services as required by law.

[Organization Name] has taken the following steps to prevent similar incidents: [list of corrective actions].

Clients who believe they may be affected are encouraged to contact [Organization Name]'s Privacy Office at [phone number] or [email] during [hours].

"[Quote from Executive Director or Privacy Officer expressing commitment to privacy and security]," said [Name, Title].

About [Organization Name]: [Two-sentence organizational description]

## Template 4: HHS Breach Report Reference

For breaches affecting 500 or more individuals, submit within 60 days via:

**HHS Breach Reporting Portal**: https://ocrportal.hhs.gov/ocr/breach/wizard_breach.jsf

Information required for the submission:
- Name of covered entity
- State
- Type of covered entity
- Contact information
- Number of individuals affected
- Date of breach (or approximate date)
- Date of discovery
- Type of breach (e.g., hacking, unauthorized access, theft, loss)
- Location of breached information (e.g., laptop, network server, email)
- Type of PHI involved
- Brief description of the breach
- Brief description of remediation

For breaches affecting fewer than 500 individuals, maintain a log and submit annually by **March 1** of the following year.
